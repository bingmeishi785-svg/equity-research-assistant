# Valuation Method Selection

Choose valuation methods based on business model, available data, maturity, profitability, and sector norms. Use multiple methods when possible.

## DCF

Use for companies with forecastable cash flows and enough historical data to estimate margins, reinvestment, and terminal value.

Key assumptions:
- Revenue growth.
- Operating margin.
- Tax rate.
- Reinvestment or capital expenditure.
- Working capital.
- Discount rate.
- Terminal growth or exit multiple.

Avoid relying on DCF alone when cash flows are highly volatile, early-stage, or driven by binary outcomes.

## DDM

Use for banks, insurers, utilities, and mature dividend-paying companies when dividends or distributable capital are central to value.

Key assumptions:
- Dividend payout.
- Sustainable return on equity.
- Cost of equity.
- Long-term growth.

## PE

Use for profitable companies where earnings quality is comparable across peers.

Check:
- Cyclicality.
- One-off items.
- Accounting differences.
- Growth and margin differences versus peers.

## PB

Use for banks, insurers, asset-heavy financials, and balance-sheet-driven businesses.

Check:
- Return on equity.
- Asset quality.
- Capital adequacy.
- Book value reliability.

## EV/EBITDA

Use for operating businesses where capital structure differs across peers and EBITDA is a meaningful proxy for operating earnings.

Check:
- Lease accounting.
- Maintenance capex.
- Working capital needs.
- EBITDA adjustments.

## EV/Sales

Use only as a secondary method for early-stage, low-margin, or temporarily unprofitable companies.

Check:
- Gross margin.
- Path to profitability.
- Revenue quality.
- Retention or repeat purchase behavior.

## Sum Of The Parts

Use when the company has materially different segments, geographies, listed stakes, or assets that deserve separate multiples.

Check:
- Segment-level revenue, profit, or assets.
- Appropriate peer set for each segment.
- Holding-company discount or conglomerate discount.

## Scenario Discipline

Always separate base, upside, and downside cases when assumptions are uncertain. State which assumptions move valuation the most.
