# Equity Research Report Structure

Use this structure for initiation reports, valuation memos, stock pitches, and company research notes. Shorter outputs can collapse sections, but should preserve the logic.

## 1. Executive Summary

State the company, ticker, region, industry, report type, and key research question. Summarize the thesis as a hypothesis, not as guaranteed advice.

Required evidence:
- Company description and segment mix.
- Current strategic issue or catalyst.
- Data sources used.

## 2. Company And Business Overview

Explain what the company sells, who pays, where revenue comes from, and which segments or geographies matter most.

Suggested charts:
- Revenue by segment.
- Revenue by geography.
- Margin by segment when available.

## 3. Industry And Competitive Position

Describe industry structure, demand drivers, regulation, supply constraints, competitive advantages, and key peers.

Suggested evidence:
- Market size and growth estimates.
- Peer comparison table.
- Pricing, volume, or capacity data.

## 4. Financial Analysis

Analyze growth, margin, cash flow, balance sheet quality, capital intensity, and working capital.

Suggested charts:
- Revenue and growth rate.
- Gross, operating, and net margins.
- Free cash flow conversion.
- Net debt or cash position.

## 5. Valuation Framework

Use more than one method when possible. Explain why each method is suitable or unsuitable for the business model.

Required outputs:
- Primary valuation method.
- Cross-check valuation method.
- Key assumptions.
- Sensitivity table or scenario discussion.

## 6. Catalysts And Monitoring Items

List events or data releases that could validate or weaken the thesis.

Examples:
- Earnings release.
- Pricing change.
- Regulatory decision.
- New product launch.
- Debt refinancing.

## 7. Risks

Include business, financial, valuation, regulatory, macro, liquidity, and data-quality risks. Explain direction and potential magnitude when possible.

## 8. Source And Verification Checklist

End with a list of sources used, missing sources, and claims that require verification before publication.
